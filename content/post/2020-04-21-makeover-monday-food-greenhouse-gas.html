Notes from original

  1. “Pound-for-pound”.
  2. Beef is bad. Beef has more total greenhouse gas emissions.
  3. A range of different approaches – “Eat Local.”, “Farm to Table”, “Raw food”, “Vegan” - have different impacts
  4. The original authors are pushing more for consumers to adopt plant-based meat, and avoid discretionary things like sugar/stimulants/alcohol.
  5. The revised article discussion focuses more on the fallacy of “Eat Local.” But even more, that highlights that our mental model of food + greenhouse gases is wrong.

Concept notes

  1. Beef is far and away the biggest greenhouse gas producer using these metrics.
  2. There are lots of pairwise comparisons for explainers. New Zealand lamb vs. beef, dairy vs. beef herd, seafood farming, etc. Each one might expose a piece of the mental model that’s missing.
  3. Can we distill down the protein message? Substitute plants generally. Believe in the Impossible (Burger). Land use is a big deal.
  4. Many ways to prioritize, not all represented (e.g. water use). Can we expose that directly? “Eat Local” vs. “Avoid processed”
  5. The metric is weird, and hard to grasp (kg of \(\text{CO}_2\))? And the kg of food product is not how we consume - especially when considering substitutes.
