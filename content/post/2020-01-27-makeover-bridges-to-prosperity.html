What works

  1. Interactivity is good. Stories in each country are very different.
  2. People and bridges in the key box are amazing testaments to impact. And divide by time for rate of impact, and it is pretty remarkable.

What doesn’t work

  1. Dry, CIA-briefing style.
  2. Some metrics like HDI and rural access index are tough to understand or score. And are they trending up – do they show our impact (more than people and bridges)?
  3. Is this better? Coko suspended footbridge
  4. Under-selling where we’d go next? “Total identified sites”, and “Estimated people without safe access” metrics.

Early thoughts

Message: 1. We’re trying to show that this activity has had a strong impact - we’re turning investment into changed lives. And we have a proven record, so re-up the investment. (Maybe where we’d go next?) 2. Country-level maps feel empty - how do we connect with these far-flung areas?

Bridge data: 1. Each bridge has a span length, and they are surprisingly long. Does this help ground the challenge of the river - size, storm surge, etc? Walking time? 2. For each bridge, there’s an estimate of population affected. Can we make this feel real? Show clusters near the river, alternative routes? Isotype? Animation of figures crossing bridges? 3. Completion year. Change to do some temporo-spatial animation?

Can we obtain: 1. Tax records? 2. Population centers? 3. Cleaned maps, high-resolution of Rwanda?
