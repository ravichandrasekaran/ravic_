Critique

What works?

What doesn’t work?

  1. Perhaps because of the constraints of print, there is a lot to look at. Each year is detailed as a small multiple, but as a reader, I’m likely to explore only a handful of birth years – my own, my child’s, and my parents. Interactivity?
  2. The outside dial doesn’t show the years under war, but the fraction of life under war. That’s a harder concept, and because the lifespans change across the figure, the fractions also change.
  3. The dual dial structure is challenging, especially without the individual example shown in the article. The inside pie is an aggregation of the outside progression.
  4. The really reductive view could be that this is a lot of ink to say that the conflict in Afghanistan is historically long.
  5. Another strange artifact is that the 3-year olds present have 100% of their lifespan in the shadow of war – but that many other 3-year olds have faced similar. Something like a survival analysis here, where we cannot foretell how the lifes unfold – but could look at it like a cohort.
